Histogram and Skewness#

Histogram#

  • Definition: A histogram is a graphical representation of the distribution of numerical data.

    • Shows shape, central tendency, and variability.

    • Approximates the probability distribution of a continuous variable.

  • Steps to Construct a Histogram:

    1. Identify the range of data (min → max).

    2. Decide number of bins (e.g., 10 bins).

    3. Compute bin size = (max – min) ÷ bins.

    4. Count frequency of values in each bin.

    5. Plot bins on the x-axis and frequency on the y-axis.

  • Relation to Probability Distribution:

    • Histogram shows empirical distribution.

    • By smoothing (using Kernel Density Estimation, KDE), we get the Probability Density Function (PDF).

Skewness#

Skewness shows how data is distributed around the mean.

Symmetrical Distribution (Normal/Gaussian)#

  • Histogram forms a bell curve.

  • Left and right sides are mirror images.

  • Mean = Median = Mode.

  • In Boxplot: median lies exactly in the center.

  • No skewness.

Right-Skewed (Positive Skew)#

  • Tail extends to the right.

  • Outliers are on the higher values side.

  • Example: Log-normal distribution.

  • Relationship: Mean > Median > Mode.

  • Boxplot: median shifts left, Q3–Q2 > Q2–Q1.

Left-Skewed (Negative Skew)#

  • Tail extends to the left.

  • Outliers are on the lower values side.

  • Relationship: Mean < Median < Mode.

  • Boxplot: median shifts right, Q2–Q1 > Q3–Q2.

Key Takeaways#

  • Histogram shows frequency distribution; KDE smoothing gives PDF.

  • Symmetrical distribution → Mean = Median = Mode.

  • Right Skew → Mean > Median > Mode.

  • Left Skew → Mean < Median < Mode.

  • Boxplots help visualize skewness and detect outliers.

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import numpy as np

# Function to generate distributions and plots
def plot_skewness_examples():
    np.random.seed(42)
    
    # Generate distributions
    normal = np.random.normal(loc=0, scale=1, size=1000)
    right_skew = np.random.exponential(scale=1, size=1000)
    left_skew = -np.random.exponential(scale=1, size=1000)
    
    data = [normal, right_skew, left_skew]
    titles = ["Symmetrical (Normal)", "Right-Skewed (Positive)", "Left-Skewed (Negative)"]
    
    fig, axes = plt.subplots(3, 2, figsize=(12, 10))
    
    for i in range(3):
        # Histogram
        axes[i, 0].hist(data[i], bins=30, edgecolor="black", alpha=0.7, density=True)
        axes[i, 0].set_title(f"Histogram: {titles[i]}")
        axes[i, 0].axvline(np.mean(data[i]), color='red', linestyle='--', label="Mean")
        axes[i, 0].axvline(np.median(data[i]), color='green', linestyle='-', label="Median")
        axes[i, 0].legend()
        
        # Boxplot
        axes[i, 1].boxplot(data[i], vert=False, patch_artist=True, boxprops=dict(facecolor="lightblue"))
        axes[i, 1].set_title(f"Boxplot: {titles[i]}")
    
    plt.tight_layout()
    plt.show()

plot_skewness_examples()
