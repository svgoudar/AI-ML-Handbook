Exponential Distribution#

The Exponential Distribution is a continuous probability distribution that models the time between independent events occurring at a constant average rate.

  • It is often used to model waiting times or lifetimes.

  • Example: The time between arrivals of buses at a station, the lifespan of a light bulb, or the time until the next customer enters a shop.

Probability Density Function (PDF)

For a rate parameter \(\lambda > 0\):

\[ f(x; \lambda) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}, \quad x \geq 0 \]

  • \(x\): the time until the next event

  • \(\lambda\): the rate (events per unit time)

  • The distribution is right-skewed (long tail to the right).

Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF)

\[ F(x; \lambda) = P(X \leq x) = 1 - e^{-\lambda x}, \quad x \geq 0 \]

This gives the probability that the waiting time is less than or equal to \(x\).

Key Properties

  1. Mean (Expected Value):

    \[ E[X] = \frac{1}{\lambda} \]

    â†’ On average, the waiting time is the reciprocal of the rate.

  2. Variance:

    \[ Var(X) = \frac{1}{\lambda^2} \]

  3. Memoryless Property:

    • The exponential distribution is memoryless:

    \[ P(X > s + t \mid X > s) = P(X > t) \]

    • Meaning: the probability of waiting another time \(t\) is independent of how much time has already passed.

    • Example: If a light bulb has been running for 1 year, the probability it survives another 6 months is the same as for a brand new bulb.

Applications

  • Reliability analysis â†’ modeling component lifetimes.

  • Queueing theory â†’ time between arrivals of customers.

  • Poisson processes â†’ the exponential distribution models the interarrival times between Poisson events.

  • Survival analysis â†’ time until death or failure.

âœ… In short:

  • The Exponential distribution models waiting times.

  • Its shape depends on the rate parameter \(\lambda\).

  • It is the only continuous distribution with the memoryless property.

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Parameters for exponential distribution
lambdas = [0.5, 1, 2]  # Different rate parameters
x = np.linspace(0, 10, 500)

plt.figure(figsize=(14,6))

# Plot PDF
plt.subplot(1,2,1)
for l in lambdas:
    pdf = l * np.exp(-l * x)
    plt.plot(x, pdf, label=f"$\lambda$={l}")
plt.title("Exponential Distribution - PDF")
plt.xlabel("x (time)")
plt.ylabel("Probability Density")
plt.legend()
plt.grid(alpha=0.3)

# Plot CDF
plt.subplot(1,2,2)
for l in lambdas:
    cdf = 1 - np.exp(-l * x)
    plt.plot(x, cdf, label=f"$\lambda$={l}")
plt.title("Exponential Distribution - CDF")
plt.xlabel("x (time)")
plt.ylabel("Cumulative Probability")
plt.legend()
plt.grid(alpha=0.3)

plt.show()
